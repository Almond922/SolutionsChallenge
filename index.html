Emergency Help

CALL EMERGENCY: 911

When to Seek Emergency Care:

  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Chest or upper abdominal pain or pressure
  • Fainting, sudden dizziness, weakness
  • Changes in vision
  • Confusion or changes in mental status
  • Any sudden or severe pain
  • Uncontrolled bleeding
  • Severe or persistent vomiting or diarrhea
  • Coughing or vomiting blood
  • Suicidal or homicidal feelings
  • Difficulty speaking or slurred speech
  • Unusual abdominal pain

While Waiting for Emergency Help:

Stay with the person who needs help. Don't leave them alone.
If they are unconscious but breathing, turn them on their side.
If there is bleeding, apply firm pressure with a clean cloth.
Do not give food or drink to someone who might need surgery.
Collect the person's medications to show emergency workers.

Common Health Issues

These are common health problems in rural areas and what to do about them:

Fever

Watch for:
  • Temperature above 100.4°F (38°C)
  • Sweating, chills, headache
  • Muscle aches, weakness
  • Loss of appetite
What to do:
  • Drink plenty of fluids
  • Rest
  • Take acetaminophen (Tylenol) or ibuprofen (Advil) as directed
  • Use a cool cloth on forehead
  • Seek medical help if: Fever is above 103°F (39.4°C), lasts more than 3 days, or is in a child under 3 months

Dehydration

Watch for:
  • Extreme thirst
  • Dry mouth and tongue
  • No tears when crying
  • No wet diapers for 3+ hours
  • Sunken eyes, cheeks
  • Sunken soft spot on top of infant's head
  • Listlessness or irritability
What to do:
  • Drink small sips of water or oral rehydration solution
  • For children, use Pedialyte or similar product
  • Avoid caffeine and alcohol
  • Homemade rehydration solution: 6 teaspoons sugar + 1/2 teaspoon salt in 1 liter of clean water
  • Seek medical help if: Person is very weak, has no urine output, or symptoms don't improve

Skin Infections

Watch for:
  • Redness, swelling, warmth
  • Pain or tenderness
  • Pus or drainage
  • Red streaks spreading from the area
  • Fever
What to do:
  • Clean with soap and water
  • Apply antibiotic ointment if available
  • Cover with clean bandage
  • Change bandage daily
  • Seek medical help if: Area gets larger, more painful, has red streaks, or you develop fever

Talk to a Doctor from Home

Free Phone Doctor Visits

Talk to a doctor right now without traveling to a clinic.

Call Doctor: 1-800-555-1234

Available: 24 hours a day, 7 days a week

Cost: FREE for rural residents

What you need: Just a phone (smart phone not required)

How Phone Doctor Visits Work:

Call the number above and tell them your name and location.
Explain your health problem to the nurse who answers.
Wait for a doctor to call you back (usually within 30 minutes).
Talk to the doctor about your symptoms and follow their advice.
If you need medicine, they can call it in to your nearest pharmacy.

Staying Healthy

Clean Water Tips

  • Boil drinking water for 1 minute if you're unsure of its safety
  • Store water in clean, covered containers
  • Use bleach to disinfect water (2 drops per quart, wait 30 minutes)
  • Keep water sources away from animal waste

Preventing Common Illnesses

  • Wash hands frequently with soap and water
  • Cover coughs and sneezes with elbow or tissue
  • Keep food preparation areas clean
  • Fully cook meat and eggs
  • Keep vaccinations up to date
  • Use mosquito nets where needed

Emergency: 911

Health Hotline: 1-800-555-HELP
